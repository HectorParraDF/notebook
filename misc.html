String theory

When dealing with the conservation of momentum $p^\mu$ for the relativistic string, it is possible to generalize the expression $p_\mu = \int_0^{\sigma_1} \mathcal{P}_\mu^\tau d\sigma$ to $p_\mu (\gamma) = \int_\gamma (\mathcal{P}_\mu^\tau - \mathcal{P}_\mu^\sigma)d\tau$, where $\gamma$ is some curve that starts on the $\sigma = 0$ boundary of the worldsheet and ends on the $\sigma = \sigma_1$ boundary. Conservation of momentum in this sense is equivalent to the invariance of the flux of $(\mathcal{P}^\tau, \mathcal{P}^\sigma)$ through $\gamma$ w.r.t this curve's shape (as long as its endpoints lie on the boundaries mentioned).
